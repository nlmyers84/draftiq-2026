What Is the DraftIQ Model
Core Philosophy — Data Over Opinion
The DraftIQ model is built entirely on what has historically predicted NFL fantasy production — not scout tape grades, not combine hype, not media consensus. Every weight is reverse-engineered from validated research across 2005–2024 draft classes. When the data conflicts with expert opinion, we go with the data.
The Two Metrics That Actually Predict WR Success
YPRR (Yards Per Route Run) is the strongest single college-to-NFL predictor for receivers — it captures route quality AND target-earning ability in one number. Breakout Age is #2 — dominating at 18-19 against older defenders is a stronger talent signal than dominating at 22. These two metrics together explain more variance than all combine drills combined.
What the Data Says to Ignore
40-yard dash in isolation — near-zero correlation with WR fantasy output when controlling for YPRR. Bench press — only marginally predictive for QBs in cold weather per Fantasy Labs data, irrelevant for skill positions. Expert tape grades — historically lower accuracy than the YPRR + BOA + dominator composite. Media consensus ADP is a sentiment measure, not a prediction.
The RB Model Is Different
For RBs, receiving YPRR out of the backfield is the #1 dynasty differentiator — pass-catching backs maintain value 3-5 years longer. BMI × speed score (weight-adjusted athleticism) is the second input. Age on draft day has extremely high historical correlation — backs drafted at 21 hit at a substantially higher rate than those drafted at 23+.
